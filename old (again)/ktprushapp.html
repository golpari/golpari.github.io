Rush App
Kappa Theta Pi Alpha Chapter
Background
Kappa Theta Pi is the nation's premiere technology fraternity. The Alpha Chapter, based at the University of Michigan - Ann Arbor, accepts a new pledge class every semester.

With so many interested students, and so many details to keep track of, all application details need to be carefully tracked.

Enter the KTP Rush App! One place for all things rush related.
Work
Designed by a team of ~8 designers and ~15 developers, the Rush App allows for fraternity brothers and rushes to create user profiles for themselves. Brothers are able to make notes on rushes in app while keeping track of events and members. Rushes can view events and learn about the existing brothers or EBoard by viewing their bios and user profiles. Account storage in Google Firebase and email verification allows for account security and editable profiles. We also implemented push notifications to notify all users of upcoming events.

As a member of both the design sub-team and development sub-team, I used Figma to design the UI and UX of the app with multiple design iterations of each screen. I used Flutter/Dart to develop different structures based off team designs. Design and development sub-teams worked together to refine final edits and complete the app flow.

I specifically worked on the design for the event listings and design of the list of EBoard members, along with iterations of other screens and the app logo. The design team and I all took part editing each others work and creating variations of different designs. On the development team, I specifically worked with a couple other teammates on developing an editable profile page. We first outlined the visuals of the screen to resemble the given design, then worked on the user interactions and back-end support.
