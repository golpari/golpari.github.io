Background
The Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory carries out a slew of research projects every minute of every day. With so many projects and quick turnarounds for many interns, a  lot of work can get lost between transitions.

One of the projects, my own, surrounded the manipulation of a robotic arm. By learning about the limitations and tasks that can be completed with a robotic arm, this research could aid other projects too.
Work
Under my research project, I learned to use translation matrices to adjust and manipulate the different axes and rotations of a robotic arm. I wrote these translations and controls into individual MATLAB scripts and functions that could be used by others for different tasks.

The MATLAB scripts were based on an open source code library and some previous work from other interns, so my work included putting together different sources of code and tracking all of my successes and failures during the creation of my scripts. I used those records for future debugging and experimenting.
